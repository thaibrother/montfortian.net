DECREE

The Brothers of St. Gabriel whose Generalate is in Rome, share in the ecclesial mission of evangelization of the world by their religious consecration , their fraternal community life and their apostolic act ivity directed towards the human and Christian education of the youth particularly of the poor. In conformity with the directives of the Second Vatican Council and other instructions of the Church, they brought out a new text "RULE OF LIFE AND CONSTITUTIONS " which the Superior General on behalf of the Chapter, presented to the Holy See requesting its approval. This Congregation for Religious and Secular Institutes, after having got it examined by its advisers, and having taken into account the favourable vote of the Congresso, by this presen t Decree approves and confirms this text with the modifications made by the same Congresso, according to the copy drafted in the French language and kept in its Archives having been observed all that should be by law.

In the Footsteps of MonHort

Louis Marie Grignion de Montfort, a Miss,i onary, lived in France, during the eighteenth century. From the very beginning of his evangelizing work among the common people, he felt called by the Holy Spirit to surround himself with disciples who should live the Gospel like him "in the footsteps of the Apostles" . To join him in his apostolate, he gathered a few Brothers and asked them to share his way of life, by the ties of obedience and poverty (1715).

LOUIS MARIE GRIGNION DE MONTFORT Portrait dating from the beginning of the XVI lith century, and kept at the Mother House of the Daughters of Wisdom at Saint Laurent sur Sevre (France).

At the moment of his death, he entrusted them to Divine Providence in order to carry on his work, particularly through "charitable schools". In 1722, the community settled at Saint-Laurent-sur-Sevre, near the community of the Daughters of Wisdom governed by Marie Louise de Jesus. The Brothers lived with the Fathers making up the community of the Missionaries of the Holy Spirit. Besides teaching in charitable schools, the Brothers devoted themselves to the works of the missions and rendering material services. When Gabriel Deshayes became Superior of the Montfortian communities, in 1821, he gave a new impe9

p. 7

tus to the Brothers; stressing particularly the importance of the teaching profession. As early as 1824, more than 40 novices took their vows of Chastity, Poverty and ·Obedience. The rapid growth in the number of the teaching Brothers led him progressively to give them a separate organization and finally to constitute them into an autonomous Institute known as the Brothers of Christian Instruction of the Holy Spirit, which became independent after his death in 1841. From then onwards, the Institute, which was legally recognized in France in 1853 under the name of the Brothers of Christian Instruction of Saint Gabriel, spread throughout the country, assuming the charge of educating the children of the working classes and undertaking also the education of the deaf and the blind. At the end of the nineteenth century, the Brothers crossed the frontiers of France to establish themselves in Canada and in several countries of Europe. Today, their missionary activity extends to all the continents in a spirit of fidelity to the Church and to their origins.

Decree of Approbation of the Institute by the Holy See

The Brothers of Christian Instruction of Saint-Gabriel, formerly called " of the Holy Ghost", whose Mother House is in the diocese of Luc;:on , and who have and invoke as their Father the Blessed Louis Marie Grignion de Montfort, humbly petitioned our Holy Father Pope Pius X that, in view of the happy propagation of their Institute to almost every country in the world, and the moral stability given to it by nearly two ce·n turies of manifold difficulties, He would graciously deign to grant to this same Institute a Decree of Praise and the Apostolic Approbation, as also a provisional approval of its Constitutions. They further begged that these three extraordinary favours might be granted conjointly without the usual obligation imposed by the laws that govern such concessions, of consulting the wishes of a Plenary Session of Cardinals . The petition was presented by the undersigned Cardinal Prefect of the Sacred Congregation of Religious at an audience held on 15th February, 1910; and His Holiness, after considering its contents and particularly the letters of recommendation from the Bishops of all the dioceses where the Brothers of Christian Instruction are engaged in their salutary work, deigned to grant to their Institute both the Decree of Praise and the Apostolic Approbation , and decreed that their Constitutions - as set out hereinbefore - be approved tentatively, saving as usual the jurisdiction of the Ordinaries. Anything to the contrary notwithstanding. 19th February, 1910. Fr. J. C. Card. Vives, Prefect. D. L. Janssens, O.S.B., Secretary

CHAPTER 7

Poor with Christ

31. Contemplate Jesus Christ, your Master: He was rich, yet for our sake, He became poor so that, through His poverty, we might become rich. He receives all things from His Father with thanksgiving: He uses, in full freedom, whatever is laid b.efore Him, He also knows when to abstain, always in fidelity to His Father who sent Him to redeem mankind. Serving your Brothers

52. Individually and as a community you are committed to a diligent search for the saving will of God and its faithful accomplishment. These structures remain flexible so as to be adaptable to the demands of the mission. 53. Since authority comes from God through men, you acknowledge its presence in your Superiors. Mindful of the dignity of the sons of God, and of the Spirit that dwells in them and makes them one, authority takes on the visage of God 's own love. Whether exercised by one person or a group it is always in the service of the Kingdom.

CHAPTER 1

The Institute of the Brothers of Saint Gabriel

1. The Institute of the Brothers of Saint Gabriel is in the Church a religious Institute of consecrated life of Pontifical right. Its beginning goes back to Saint Louis Marie de Montfort and it received the approbation of the Holy See on the 19th of February 1910. Its members dedicate themselves to God by the profession of the evangelical counsels lived out in fraternal communities. The Institute devotes itself mainly to the mission of education. 2. The Institute is recognized by the Church as a lay Institute in line with a legitimate tradition. However, it may have some of its members admitted to Holy Orders "to supply needed priestly ministration for its own 'houses'". S 2 - The Institute considers that the word 'houses' applies here to its communities and to their mission.

3. In view of the lay character of the Institute, the functions of Superior General and Vicar General are not entrusted to Brothers ordained to the priestly ministry.

p. 32

4. The Institute manifests its irrevocable attachment to the Church by its respect for and its obedience to the Pope and its loyal co-operation with the Bishops. 5. The spirit of the Institute is based on the spirituality and the missionary dynamism of Saint Louis Marie de Montfort. 6. The Rosary and the practice of the perfect Devotion to the Blessed Virgin, the secret of holiness taught by Saint Louis Marie de Montfort, form an essential part of the patrimony of the Institute. The total consecration to Jesus through Mary is the fundamental feature of the Marian life of its members. 7. The patronal feast of the Institute is the Annunciation. Thus will the Brothers celebrate the mystery of the presence of the Son of God in the lives of men, and the privileged role of Mary in the plan of salvation. 8. The Institute of the Brothers of Saint Gabriel, by its origin and history, belongs to the Montfortian family; hence its privileged relationship and co-operation with the Missionaries of the Company of Mary and the Daughters of Wisdom. CHAPTER 14

Rule of Life, Constitutions and General Statutes

The Rule of Life and the Constitutions express the spirituality and charism of the Institute. The Rule of Life lays down the basic orientations of the Brother's life; the norms that are the implementation of it are defined by the Constitutions. These texts may be altered by the General Chapter only by a majority of two thirds of its members and with the approval from the Holy See .
