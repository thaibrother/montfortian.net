49 Martyrs of the Brothers of Saint Gabriel
Catalonia · 1936

Pope Leo XIV has recognized the martyrdom of forty-nine Brothers of Saint Gabriel together with one diocesan priest, killed in hatred of the faith between July and November 1936 in Catalonia, Spain.

A milestone toward Beatification

The Decree

On Monday, 27 April 2026, during an audience with Cardinal Marcello Semeraro, Prefect of the Dicastery for the Causes of Saints, Pope Leo XIV authorized the promulgation of decrees concerning martyrdom, the offering of life, and the heroic virtues of several Servants of God.

Among them is the recognition of the martyrdom of the Servant of God Stanislao Ortega García (born Lorenzo) and 48 companions, members of the Institute of the Brothers of Christian Instruction of Saint Gabriel, together with the diocesan priest Emanuel Berenguer Clusella.

They were killed between July and November 1936 in various locations of Catalonia, Spain, during the same period of religious persecution — in hatred of the faith. — Vatican News, 27 April 2026

Historical Context

1936 — the Spanish Civil War began in July, ushering in what historians call the “Red Terror” in which Catholic religious and clergy were persecuted on an unprecedented scale.

Across Spain, approximately 6,832 Catholic clergy and religious were killed — including 13 bishops, nearly 4,200 diocesan priests, and thousands of religious men and women. It was the largest religious persecution of the 20th century in Western Europe.

In Barcelona alone, 46 of the 49 Gabrielite Brothers were murdered, sharing the fate of religious from many congregations whose lives and educational apostolate were rooted in Catalonia.

The Cause for Beatification of these Servants of God was opened in 2001. After 25 years of canonical examination, Pope Leo XIV recognized their martyrdom on 27 April 2026 — a decisive step toward their proclamation as Blesseds.

For the Montfortian family

One day before the 310th anniversary of the death of our Founder, Saint Louis-Marie de Montfort, the Church has given us a gift: the recognition that 49 of our brothers, heirs to his charism, stood firm in faith to the very end.

They did not choose to be saints — they chose to be Brothers of Saint Gabriel faithfully: teaching the young, serving the poor, consecrating themselves to Jesus through Mary — until the day when everything had to be witnessed in flesh and blood, and they remained who they were.
