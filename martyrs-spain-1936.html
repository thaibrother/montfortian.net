📜 The Decree of 27 April 2026

On Monday, 27 April 2026, His Holiness Pope Leo XIV authorized the promulgation of decrees recognizing the martyrdom, offering of life, and heroic virtues of several Servants of God.

Cardinal Marcello Semeraro, Prefect of the Dicastery for the Causes of Saints, was received in audience to present these decrees.

Among the decrees promulgated on that day, one brought profound joy to the Brothers of Saint Gabriel worldwide, including the Province of Thailand.

✨ Those Recognized — 49 Brothers + 1 Diocesan Priest

The decree recognizes the martyrdom of:

  • Bro. Stanislao Ortega García (born Lorenzo) — leader of the group
  • 48 fellow Brothers of the Institute of the Brothers of Christian Instruction of Saint Gabriel
  • Fr. Emanuel Berenguer Clusella — diocesan priest and chaplain

A total of 50 Servants of God who gave their lives in witness to the faith of Christ.

🕯️ Ninety Years Ago

They were killed between July and November 1936 in various locations across Catalonia, Spain, amid the religious persecution and hatred of the faith (in odium fidei) that marked the early months of the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939).

It was one of the most violent persecutions of the Church in the 20th century — an estimated 6,832 religious and clergy were killed across Spain in that brief period.

Threatened with death, the 49 Brothers and their chaplain did not deny their faith, did not abandon the Lord, and did not forsake their vocation as Brothers entrusted with the formation of youth according to the spirit of Saint Louis-Marie Grignion de Montfort, our Founder.

📚 Historical Context of the Cause

According to the records of the Brothers of Saint Gabriel, the Cause for Beatification of the 49 Brothers and their chaplain — all killed between July and November 1936 — was opened in 2001.

The diocesan phase was conducted at the Archdiocese of Barcelona and concluded in 2005, after the gathering of testimonies and documentary evidence concerning the circumstances of their deaths.

The dossier was then forwarded to Rome. Finally, on 27 April 2026, His Holiness Pope Leo XIV granted official recognition of their martyrdom — opening the way to their proclamation as Blesseds (Beatification), the first step toward Canonization.
